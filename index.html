Jeri E. Wieringa

PhD Candidate in History at George Mason University. My dissertation is using digital methods to investigate and engage the intersection of health reform, Seventh-day Adventism, and gender in the 19th century.

Religious History

As a historian of American Religion, I focus on millennialism and reform movements, with particular attention to gender and social systems.

Digital History

As a digital historian, I use computational methods while considering the ways digital technology challenges and changes how we approach and communicate about the past.

Teaching

From workshops to standard courses, I focus on teaching technical skills and computational thinking within the context of humanities research.
